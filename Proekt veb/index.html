biography

Hello, everyone! My name is Damjan Karadakoski, and I was born on July 29, 2003, in Ohrid, Macedonia. From a young age, I discovered my passion for Taekwondo. Through countless hours of training and unwavering dedication, I achieved notable success in the sport, earning several medals that served as a testament to my skill and determination.

As I grew older, my thirst for new experiences led me to the exhilaration of skiing down snowy slopes and the serenity of swimming in clear waters. Skiing provided an adrenaline rush like no other, while swimming offered a tranquil escape and a profound connection to nature. These activities became integral parts of my life, allowing me to embrace the beauty and excitement of outdoor sports.

During my childhood, I had an incredible time riding bikes with my friends. Exploring the streets of our neighborhood, feeling the wind against our faces, and sharing laughter and joy created unforgettable memories. These bike rides not only fostered lasting friendships but also ignited a deep love for outdoor adventures and the simple pleasures of life.

Alongside my physical pursuits, I developed a deep passion for computers and technology. The world of coding, software development, and the boundless possibilities of the digital realm fascinated me. Fuelled by this passion, I aspire to study computer engineering and transform my love for computers into a fulfilling career.

Travel

Dubrovnik

Dubrovnik, known as the "Pearl of the Adriatic," is a captivating coastal city in southern Croatia. Its ancient city walls, surrounding the historic Old Town, offer panoramic views of the Adriatic Sea. The elegant limestone buildings along the Stradun, the main street, house charming shops and cafes. Landmarks like the Rector's Palace, Dubrovnik Cathedral, and Sponza Palace reveal the city's rich history. Cultural festivals, vibrant nightlife, and nearby natural wonders like Lokrum Island and the Elaphiti Islands make Dubrovnik an enchanting destination that combines history, culture, and natural beauty.

Perast

Perast, Montenegro, is a picturesque coastal town nestled on the Bay of Kotor. With its well-preserved architecture and stunning natural surroundings, including iconic islets, Perast offers a peaceful retreat. Visitors can explore historic landmarks, enjoy waterfront dining, and embark on boat trips to nearby islands. Time seems to stand still in Perast, inviting travelers to immerse themselves in its beauty and tranquility.

Thessaloniki

Thessaloniki, Greece, is a vibrant city brimming with history, culture, and energy. As the second-largest city in the country, it offers a unique blend of ancient ruins, Byzantine architecture, and modern attractions. From the iconic White Tower overlooking the Thermaic Gulf to the bustling Aristotelous Square, Thessaloniki captivates visitors with its charm. Explore its archaeological sites, indulge in delicious Greek cuisine, and wander through the vibrant markets and lively neighborhoods. With a rich history and a vibrant atmosphere, Thessaloniki invites you to experience its captivating blend of old and new.
